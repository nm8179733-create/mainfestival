📖 About

Govardhan Puja falls on the day immediately after the main Diwali night — Kartika Shukla Pratipada. It celebrates one of the most beloved stories in Krishna's life: the day he lifted Govardhan Hill on his little finger to shelter the people of Vrindavan from Indra's devastating seven-day storm.

The people of Vrindavan traditionally worshipped Indra — the king of the Gods and God of rain — with elaborate rituals to ensure good harvests. One year the young Krishna asked his father Nanda: "Why do you worship Indra? It is not Indra who sustains you. It is this Govardhan hill — that gives your cows grass, water from its streams, shade and shelter, and the very earth beneath your feet. Worship what actually nourishes you."

The villagers agreed and redirected their worship to Govardhan hill. Indra was furious. In his arrogance he unleashed seven days and nights of catastrophic rain upon Vrindavan.

Krishna calmly walked to Govardhan hill and lifted the entire mountain on his little finger — holding it like an umbrella over the entire village for seven days. Every person, every cow, every animal sheltered beneath it. Not a drop of rain touched them. On the seventh day Indra's pride was shattered — he descended from heaven and bowed before Krishna.

⭐ Significance

Indra represents ego — the arrogance of believing that power makes you worthy of worship. Krishna represents the truth: that real greatness is the willingness to protect the weak, to lift what is heavy, to shelter others from the storm. The mountain was lifted not by divine power but by love. And love is always stronger than storm. Govardhan Puja is the day we honor nature as divine — the hill, the cow, the earth that actually feeds and sustains us.

🏔️ Making the Govardhan — From Cow Dung

✦ Essential Element
Getting Fresh Cow Dung

Fresh cow dung is the central ritual element of this puja. Available from any goshaala (cow shelter) or dairy near you. In cities, many temples distribute cow dung specifically for Govardhan Puja — ask at your nearest temple or goshaala a day in advance. In villages, it is collected fresh on the morning of the puja.

✦ Main Structure
Shape the Hill

At the entrance of your home or in your courtyard, shape the cow dung into a long mound or hill form on the ground — approximately 1–2 feet long. Decorate it with fresh flowers, especially marigold, pressed into the surface. Create indentations representing the features of a hill. At the center, create a small reclining figure from cow dung representing Krishna resting after lifting the hill.

✦ Surrounding Figures
Create the Scene of Vrindavan

Around the Govardhan mound, arrange small cow dung figures representing: cows and calves, Gopas (cowherds), Gopis (cowherd women), and trees. Decorate the entire arrangement with marigold garlands, fresh flowers, and rangoli around the border. Place diyas all around it — the scene of Vrindavan sheltered under the hill, brought to life at your threshold.

✦ Why Cow Dung
The Sacred Earth

Cow dung is considered sacred in Indian tradition — it is the gift of the cow, the most sacred animal in this story. Using it to create Govardhan hill connects the ritual to the very earth and nature it honors. Govardhan hill is the symbol of nature's sustaining grace — and cow dung is nature's most direct blessing in an agricultural community.

🎭 The Complete Govardhan Puja

✦ Step 1
Worship the Govardhan Mound

Go to the cow dung Govardhan at your entrance. Light incense and a diya. Sprinkle clean water around it. Apply kumkum and chandan on the mound. Apply turmeric. Offer fresh marigold flowers at its base. Fold hands and bow before it as before a sacred hill.

"Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya" — 108 times
✦ Step 2 — Most Important
Pradakshina — Circle the Hill 7 Times

Walk clockwise around the Govardhan mound 7 times — representing the 7 days Krishna held the hill. Keep your right side toward the mound. Walk as an entire family together — this is one of the most joyful moments of Govardhan Puja. As you walk, chant:

"Jai Giriraj Maharaj Ki Jai — Jai Govardhan Maharaj Ki Jai"
✦ Step 3
Govardhan Prayer & Personal Bow

After the 7 circles, bow completely before the mound and offer your prayer: "O Govardhan — you sheltered everyone who needed shelter. Bless my home with the same abundance and protection." Then recite the Govardhan prayer — the most specific and meaningful prayer of this day.

"Govardhana Dhara Vishno, Girirajaya Te Namaha — Nanda Gopa Mahananda, Govinda Gopala Palaka"
✦ Step 4 — Heart of Puja
Annakut Offering to Krishna

Go to the Annakut (mountain of food). Light diya and incense before the Krishna idol. Offer tulsi leaves — essential for Krishna puja. Apply chandan tilak on the idol. Offer lotus or yellow flowers. Chant "Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya" 108 times before the mountain of food. Perform full Aarti with camphor, waving clockwise while singing "Jai Jagdish Hare."

✦ Step 5 — Most Essential Act
Distribute Everything — Feed Everyone

After Aarti, the entire Annakut becomes prasad. Distribute ALL the food to family, neighbors, and the poor. No food from the Annakut is kept back. This distribution is the most important act of Govardhan Puja — it is not a festival of worshipping food but of feeding everyone. The mountain of food must become empty by the end of the day.

✦ Step 6
Gau Puja — Worship the Cow

The cow is central to this story — Krishna was a cowherd, Govardhan fed the cows, the cows sustained Vrindavan. If you have access to a cow at a goshaala or temple: decorate with marigold garland, apply kumkum tilak on her forehead, apply turmeric on her hooves, chandan on her sides. Offer fresh grass and jaggery. Wave a diya before her and bow.

"Om Surabhi Namaste Devi, Vishnu Vaksha Sthalasthite — Sarva Deva Namaskaram, Gavam Madhye Vasas Tava"

🍱 The Annakut — Mountain of Food

Annakut means "mountain of food." Traditionally 56 items (Chappan Bhog) are prepared — the number comes from Krishna eating 8 meals a day across the 7 days he held up Govardhan: 8 × 7 = 56. During those seven days he fasted while sheltering everyone. The 56 items are offered in gratitude for those 56 meals he missed. All food is arranged on a raised platform in a mountain shape, piled high. No onion or garlic in anything.

🗺️ How It Is Celebrated

Mathura & Vrindavan
The Sacred Center

Lakhs of devotees perform the 21 km Govardhan Parikrama — circumambulating the actual Govardhan hill on foot. This is one of the most sacred acts in Vaishnavism. The hill is decorated, worshipped, and covered in flowers. Some devotees perform the parikrama by doing full-body prostrations — lying flat, marking the spot, rising, walking to the mark, lying flat again — for the entire 21 km.

Nathdwara, Rajasthan
Shrinathji Temple

The Shrinathji temple at Nathdwara — home to the most sacred Krishna idol in the Pushtimarg tradition — holds the grandest Annakut in India. Thousands of food items are arranged in a room-filling mountain. The spectacle of the Nathdwara Annakut is considered without parallel.

ISKCON Temples Worldwide
Grand Annakut

ISKCON temples hold particularly spectacular Annakut celebrations open to all. Hundreds of food preparations are offered to Krishna and then distributed as prasad. Visiting any ISKCON temple on this day — in any city in India or the world — is deeply recommended.

Udupi, Karnataka
South Indian Tradition

The Udupi Krishna Matha — one of the oldest Krishna temples in India — celebrates Annakut with the unique South Indian devotional tradition of the Madhva school, with elaborate food offerings specific to this tradition.

Every Home Across India
The Neighborhood Govardhan

Across every Krishna-worshipping household in North India — the cow dung Govardhan mound appears at the entrance on this morning. Neighbors come to do pradakshina together, children make the small figures, and the entire neighborhood Annakut is prepared and shared.

🙏 Key Prayers & Mantras

🏔️
Govardhan Prayer — Said During Pradakshina & Puja
"Govardhana Dhara Vishno, Girirajaya Te Namaha — Nanda Gopa Mahananda, Govinda Gopala Palaka"
O Vishnu who lifted Govardhan — salutation to the king of mountains. O source of Nanda's great joy, O Govinda — protector of cows and cowherds. The most specific and meaningful prayer of this day, said while circling the mound all 7 times.
🐄
Cow Worship Mantra — Said During Gau Puja
"Om Surabhi Namaste Devi, Vishnu Vaksha Sthalasthite — Sarva Deva Namaskaram, Gavam Madhye Vasas Tava"
Salutation to Surabhi (the divine cow), who rests on Vishnu's chest. All Gods bow to you — you reside among the cows. Said while applying kumkum on the cow's forehead and offering fresh grass. The cow is not merely an animal here — she is the living symbol of nature's sustaining grace.
🙌
Circling Chant — Said With Each Step Around the Mound
"Jai Giriraj Maharaj Ki Jai — Jai Govardhan Maharaj Ki Jai"
Victory to the Great King of Mountains — Victory to Govardhan Maharaj! Called out joyfully with each of the 7 pradakshina rounds. The entire family chants together as they walk. The 7 rounds representing the 7 days Krishna held the mountain aloft.
✨
Primary Krishna Mantra — 108 Times Before Annakut
"Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya"
I bow to Lord Vasudeva (Krishna). The primary mantra of Krishna devotion — chanted 108 times before the mountain of food, and again during Pradakshina. The same mantra used during Holika Dahan, it binds all Vishnu traditions in one sound.

📌 Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do's

  • Make the Govardhan mound from cow dung — even a small one at home is complete
  • Prepare the Annakut — as many varieties as possible, piled into a mountain shape
  • Perform Pradakshina 7 times with the whole family — circling and chanting together
  • Worship the cow — visit a goshaala or temple cow
  • Distribute ALL Annakut food as prasad — feed as many people as possible
  • Offer fresh white butter (makhan) to Krishna — always
  • Visit a Krishna or ISKCON temple for their grand Annakut
  • If near Mathura/Vrindavan — do the Govardhan Parikrama (21 km)
  • Reflect on where Indra's arrogance — ego demanding worship it doesn't deserve — operates in your own life

✕ Don'ts

  • Do not keep Annakut food for yourself only — it must be distributed, all of it
  • Do not offer onion or garlic in any food prepared for Krishna
  • Do not skip the cow worship — the cow is central to this festival's meaning
  • Do not treat this as merely "the day after Diwali" — Govardhan Puja has its own profound significance
  • Do not use non-vegetarian food in any Annakut preparation

A child lifted a mountain on his little finger — not to show power, but to prove a point. That love protects better than power. That nature sustains better than pride. That the hill beneath your feet deserves more gratitude than the king in the sky who demands it.
