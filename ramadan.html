✦ What & Why

The Month the Quran Descended

🌙

Ramadan is the holiest month in Islam — the month in which the Holy Quran was first revealed to Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) by Allah through the Angel Jibreel (Gabriel). This revelation began on a night called Laylat ul-Qadr (the Night of Power), believed to fall in the last 10 odd nights of Ramadan.

Sawm — fasting — is one of the Five Pillars of Islam, obligatory for every adult Muslim who is physically capable. But Ramadan goes far beyond abstaining from food. It is a month of Taqwa (God-consciousness), Sabr (patience), Shukr (gratitude), intense Quran recitation, repentance, and profound generosity.

It is believed that during Ramadan, the gates of Heaven are opened, the gates of Hell are closed, and the Shayateen (devils) are chained — making it the greatest month for spiritual growth in the entire year.

🕌

Taqwa — God-Consciousness

Being constantly aware of Allah in every moment — in speech, thought, deed, and silence. Ramadan sharpens this awareness like no other month.

🤲

Sabr — Patience & Self-Discipline

Hunger, thirst, fatigue — all endured willingly for Allah. Ramadan is the greatest annual training in the mastery of the self.

💰

Zakat & Sadaqah — Giving

Charity is multiplied in reward during Ramadan. Feeding the poor, sponsoring Iftars, and paying Zakat ul-Fitr are central obligations of the month.

📖

Quran — Deep Connection

The tradition is to complete the entire Quran once during Ramadan — one Juz (section) per day over 30 days — in Arabic and in translation.

🌙

Tawbah — Repentance

The month is an annual spiritual reset — seeking sincere forgiveness for all past sins and committing to a better self going forward.

The month of Ramadan is that in which was revealed the Quran — a guidance for mankind, and clear proofs of the guidance, and the criterion. So whoever witnesses the month, let him fast it. — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:185

— The Holy Quran

✦ The Sacred Daily Cycle

A Day in Ramadan — From Suhoor to Isha

🌑

Before Fajr — Pre-Dawn

Suhoor — The Blessed Pre-Dawn Meal

Eaten before the Fajr Adhan (call to prayer), after which the fast officially begins. The Prophet (PBUH) said: "Take Suhoor, for in Suhoor there is blessing." Eating Suhoor with the sincere intention of fasting for Allah is itself an act of worship. Choose slow-energy foods: dates, oats, eggs, yogurt, nuts, whole grains, and plenty of water.

"Wa bisawmi ghadinn nawaiytu min shahri Ramadan"

Meaning: "I intend to fast tomorrow for the month of Ramadan." — Make this Niyyah (intention) before each fast.

🌅

At True Dawn

Fajr Prayer — 2 Rakats

The first of five obligatory daily prayers. Perform Wudu (ritual ablution — washing hands, mouth, nose, face, arms, head, ears, and feet in sequence) then stand on the prayer mat facing Qibla (direction of Mecca). Fajr consists of 2 Rakats. Begin with Allahu Akbar, recite Surah Al-Fatiha followed by a short Surah in each Rakat, perform Ruku (bowing), Sujood (prostration), and close with Tasleem — turning right then left saying As-salamu 'alaykum wa rahmatullah.

☀️

Through the Fasting Day

Quran, Dhikr & Guarding Character

Read one Juz of Quran daily to complete the full text over 30 days. Throughout the day, keep the tongue moist with dhikr (remembrance of Allah): Subhanallah, Alhamdulillah, Allahu Akbar, La ilaha illallah, Astaghfirullah. Perform Dhuhr (4 Rakats) at noon and Asr (4 Rakats) in the afternoon. Guard your character — no lying, backbiting, arguments, or foul language. The Prophet (PBUH) said fasting without guarding the tongue misses the point entirely.

Rakats per prayer: Fajr = 2 · Dhuhr = 4 · Asr = 4 · Maghrib = 3 · Isha = 4
🌇

At Sunset — The Most Awaited Moment

Maghrib — Iftar, Breaking the Fast

The fast breaks at the Maghrib Adhan. The Sunnah: break first with a fresh date and water. Then pray Maghrib (3 Rakats) before the full meal. Share the Iftar with family, neighbors, and the poor. The Prophet (PBUH) said: "Whoever feeds a fasting person will have a reward equal to his." Traditional Iftar: dates, fruit chaat, samosas, haleem, sherbets, biryani, kebabs, and falooda.

"Allahumma inni laka sumtu wa bika amantu wa 'alayka tawakkaltu wa 'ala rizqika aftartu"

Meaning: "O Allah, I fasted for You, I believe in You, I put my trust in You, and I break my fast with Your provision."

🌌

Night — After Isha

Taraweeh — The Special Night Prayer of Ramadan

After Isha (4 Rakats), Taraweeh — 8 or 20 voluntary extra Rakats performed only in Ramadan — is prayed at the mosque. A Hafiz (one who has memorized the entire Quran) leads the congregation, reciting portions each night so the full Quran is completed over 30 nights. Listening to the entire Quran recited in Taraweeh is a profound experience. The Prophet (PBUH) said: "Whoever prays during the nights of Ramadan with faith and seeking reward, his previous sins will be forgiven."

✦ How to Pray

The Steps of Salah

01

Wudu — Ritual Purification

Washing hands · mouth · nose · face · arms · head · ears · feet

Begin with Bismillah and intention. Wash each limb in sequence, right before left, three times each. Wudu is broken by using the bathroom, passing gas, bleeding, or sleep — must be renewed before prayer.

02

Takbir — Opening the Prayer

"Allahu Akbar" — Allah is the Greatest

Raise both hands to ear level, palms forward, and say Allahu Akbar. You have entered a sacred state. Do not speak, look around, or break concentration from this point until Tasleem.

03

Qiyam — Surah Al-Fatiha

Right hand over left on chest. Recite Thana, then Al-Fatiha, then a short Surah.

Al-Fatiha is the cornerstone — recited 17 times daily across all prayers. Surah Al-Ikhlas is the most commonly recited short Surah for beginners. Say Ameen after Al-Fatiha.

04

Ruku — The Bow

"Subhana Rabbiyal Azeem" — Glory to my Lord the Most Great

Bow at the waist, hands on knees, back flat, head level with back. Recite 3 times. Rise saying Sami Allahu liman hamidah, then Rabbana lakal hamd.

05

Sujood — The Prostration

"Subhana Rabbiyal A'la" — Glory to my Lord the Most High

The closest a human is to Allah. Forehead, nose, both palms, both knees, and toes all touch the ground. Recite 3 times. Make personal dua here — this is the most powerful position in prayer.

06

Tasleem — Closing the Prayer

"As-salamu 'alaykum wa rahmatullah"

After the final Tashahhud and Darood Ibrahim, turn your face to the right and recite, then to the left. The prayer is complete. Now make personal dua — speak to Allah in any language from your heart.

✦ The Most Powerful Night of the Year ✦

Laylat ul-Qadr — The Night of Power

"The Night of Power is better than a thousand months."

— Surah Al-Qadr 97:3

Worship on this single night equals the reward of over 83 years of continuous worship. It falls on one of the odd nights of the last 10 days of Ramadan — the 21st, 23rd, 25th, 27th, or 29th — with the 27th most commonly believed, though the exact night is intentionally hidden so that Muslims worship intensely across all of them. The Prophet (PBUH) would stay awake the entire night in prayer, Quran recitation, and dua. Consider performing I'tikaf — spiritual seclusion in the mosque — for the last 10 days.

The Dua of Laylat ul-Qadr — taught by the Prophet (PBUH)

"Allahumma innaka 'afuwwun tuhibbul 'afwa fa'fu 'anni"

"O Allah, You are the Pardoner and You love to pardon, so pardon me." — Repeat abundantly on every odd night of the last 10 days.

✦ Islam Is Mercy

Who Is Exempt from Fasting?

🧒

Children

Below the age of puberty. Fasting is not obligatory, but gentle encouragement as they grow is recommended.

🤒

The Ill

Those whose health would be seriously worsened by fasting. Must make up (Qada) missed fasts after Ramadan when recovered.

🤰

Pregnant Women

If fasting would harm the mother or baby. Must make up fasts or pay Fidyah (feeding one poor person per missed day).

🤱

Breastfeeding Mothers

If fasting would harm milk supply or the infant. Same Qada or Fidyah as above applies.

✈️

Travelers

Those on a journey may break the fast and make it up later after returning.

👵

The Elderly & Chronically Ill

Permanently unable to fast. Pay Fidyah instead of Qada for each missed day.

✦ Sacred Supplications

Essential Duas of Ramadan

Iftar Dua — At the Moment of Breaking Fast

Allahumma inni laka sumtu wa bika amantu wa 'alayka tawakkaltu wa 'ala rizqika aftartu

"O Allah, I fasted for You, I believe in You, I put my trust in You, and I break my fast with Your provision."

Laylat ul-Qadr Dua — Repeat Every Odd Night

Allahumma innaka 'afuwwun tuhibbul 'afwa fa'fu 'anni

"O Allah, You are the Pardoner and You love to pardon, so pardon me." — The specific dua the Prophet (PBUH) taught for the Night of Power.

Dua for Entering the Mosque

Allahumma iftah li abwaba rahmatik

"O Allah, open for me the gates of Your mercy." — Said upon entering the masjid every time during Ramadan and beyond.

Dua for Forgiveness — Say Abundantly

Astaghfirullaha Rabbi min kulli zambin wa atubu ilayh

"I seek forgiveness from Allah, my Lord, for all my sins and I turn to Him in repentance." — The most important Ramadan habit outside of prayer.

Niyyah — Daily Fasting Intention

Wa bisawmi ghadinn nawaiytu min shahri Ramadan

"I intend to fast tomorrow for the month of Ramadan." — Made each night or at Suhoor. The intention is in the heart; saying it aloud is permissible.

Eid Greeting

Taqabbal Allahu minna wa minkum

"May Allah accept from us and from you." — The traditional Eid greeting exchanged after the Eid prayer, affirming that all the worship of Ramadan is offered to Allah alone.

✦ Sacred Guidelines

Do's & Don'ts of Ramadan

✓

Do's

  • 🌙

    Make sincere Niyyah every day before Fajr

    Intention is the foundation of every act of worship in Islam. A Ramadan fast without intention is not valid. Make it in your heart each night or at Suhoor.

  • 🌑

    Eat Suhoor — never skip it

    The Prophet (PBUH) specifically said there is barakah (blessing) in Suhoor. It sustains you through the fast and is itself a worshipful act done with intention.

  • 🌴

    Break fast with dates and water — follow the Sunnah

    This is the way of the Prophet (PBUH). A single date and sip of water before Maghrib prayer is the Sunnah. Pray Maghrib before the full Iftar meal.

  • 🕌

    Pray all 5 daily prayers on time, every day

    Salah is the pillar of the religion. Missing prayers during Ramadan defeats its purpose. Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha — all five, every day, on time.

  • 🤲

    Attend Taraweeh prayers at the mosque

    Taraweeh is one of the most spiritually beautiful experiences of Ramadan. Hearing the entire Quran recited over 30 nights in congregation is irreplaceable.

  • 📖

    Read at least one Juz of Quran daily

    The Quran and Ramadan are inseparable. One Juz per day completes the full Quran in 30 days. Read in Arabic and follow with translation to understand the meaning.

  • 💰

    Give generously in charity — Zakat and Sadaqah

    Rewards for charity are multiplied in Ramadan. Feed the poor, sponsor Iftars, pay Zakat ul-Fitr before Eid. The Prophet was described as "more generous than the blowing wind" during this month.

  • ⭐

    Intensify worship in the last 10 nights

    Seek Laylat ul-Qadr on all odd nights (21st, 23rd, 25th, 27th, 29th). Stay awake in prayer, Quran, and dua. The reward of one night equals 83+ years of worship.

  • 💳

    Pay Zakat ul-Fitr before the Eid prayer

    Obligatory for every household member — ensures even the poor can celebrate Eid with food. Approximately the cost of one meal per person. Pay before the Eid Salah begins.

✗

Don'ts

  • 🚫

    Do not eat, drink, or smoke from Fajr to Maghrib

    This is the fast — total abstention from all food, drink (including water), and smoking from the Fajr Adhan to the Maghrib Adhan. Intentional breaking requires Kaffarah (expiation).

  • 😴

    Do not waste the day in excessive sleep or entertainment

    Ramadan hours are finite and irreplaceable. Every hour spent sleeping through the day or in mindless entertainment is an hour of multiplied reward lost forever.

  • 🗣️

    Do not lie, argue, backbite, or use foul language

    The Prophet (PBUH) said Allah has no need for the person's hunger if they cannot give up false speech. Fasting the tongue and character is as essential as fasting the stomach.

  • 🕌

    Do not miss any of the 5 daily prayers

    Missing obligatory prayer in the holiest month is a severe loss. If you miss a prayer due to sleep or forgetfulness, make it up immediately when you remember — there is no excuse to abandon it.

  • 🌙

    Do not fast on Eid day — it is Haram

    Fasting on Eid ul-Fitr (the day immediately after Ramadan) is explicitly forbidden in Islam. Eat before the Eid prayer. It is a day of celebration and gratitude, not fasting.

  • 🍽️

    Do not be wasteful with food at Iftar

    Ramadan is a month of gratitude and empathy for the hungry. Piling up excessive food while millions go without is a contradiction of everything the fast is meant to teach.

  • 📱

    Do not neglect Taraweeh or the last 10 nights

    Missing Taraweeh or sleeping through the last 10 nights is one of the greatest regrets a person can have about Ramadan. These nights will not return for a full year — and you may not be here for them.
