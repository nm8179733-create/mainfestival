Chhath Puja

The Most Ancient Sun Festival

Gratitude, Purity & the Worship of Life's Source

October 25-28, 2026 Bihar, Jharkhand, UP
Scroll to explore
⭐

Significance

New Year symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the opportunity for growth. It fosters unity as people from diverse backgrounds come together to celebrate.

📅

When is it?

Chhath Puja is a 4-day festival beginning on the 4th day (Chaturthi) of the bright fortnight (Shukla Paksha) of the Hindu month of Kartika — four days after Diwali.

In 2026: October 25th–28th

Check drikpanchang.com for exact Sandhya Arghya and Usha Arghya timings for your city — these are the most critical timings of the entire festival.

🌟

What and Why?

Chhath Puja is the worship of Surya (the Sun God) and his consort Chhathi Maiya (Goddess Shashti) — the sixth form of Devi and the divine mother of life. It is one of the oldest surviving Vedic festivals — predating even most temple-based Hindu worship.

What makes Chhath unique:

🌅

Setting Sun Worship

The only Hindu festival where the setting sun is specifically worshipped — honoring the sun even as it descends

💧

Standing in Water

Requires standing in water for extended periods during offerings — connecting directly with nature

🙏

No Priests

The devotee worships directly — no intermediary needed. The most democratic of all Hindu pujas

⏱️

Rigorous Fast

36 hours of complete Nirjala (no food, no water) fast — one of the most rigorous in Hinduism

📖

The Stories Behind Chhath

👸

Draupadi's Chhath

When the Pandavas were in exile and in desperate need, Draupadi observed Chhath Puja on the advice of the sage Dhaumya — worshipping the Sun God for the restoration of their kingdom and wellbeing. Her prayers were answered.

⚔️

Karna's Chhath

Karna — the great warrior son of Surya in the Mahabharata — worshipped his divine father every day by standing waist-deep in water offering Arghya to the sun. Chhath's standing-in-water tradition is directly connected to Karna's daily worship.

🏹

Rama and Sita's Chhath

After returning from Lanka, Rama and Sita observed Chhath on Kartika Shukla Shashti — worshipping the sun together. This is why the main day of Chhath is called Shashti (the sixth day).

📅

The 4 Days Journey

1

Nahay Khay

Bath and Eat

The purification day. Take a complete ritual bath in sacred water. Eat one simple sattvic meal — Chana dal and Lauki with rice. After this meal, the Nirjala fast begins.

🛁 Ritual Bath 🍚 One Sattvic Meal 🧘 Begin Purity
2

Kharna

Fasting and Evening Prasad

Complete Nirjala fast all day. In the evening, break the fast with Kharna Prasad (Rasiyaav - rice cooked in jaggery and milk). After this, no food or water for 36 hours.

🚫 No Food/Water 🌅 Evening Prasad ⏱️ 36hr Fast Begins
3

Sandhya Arghya

Evening Offering to Setting Sun

The most spectacular day. Prepare the Soop (bamboo basket) with offerings. Go to the ghat before sunset. Stand waist-deep in water and offer Arghya to the setting sun.

🧺 Prepare Soop 🌊 Water Offering 🌅 Setting Sun

The only festival that worships the setting sun — gratitude even in its fading

4

Usha Arghya

Morning Offering to Rising Sun

Wake before dawn. Return to the ghat. Offer Arghya to the rising sun. Break the 36-hour fast with prasad. The most sacred and powerful moment of the festival.

🌄 Pre-dawn ☀️ Rising Sun 🍽️ Break Fast

Standing in water at sunrise after 36 hours fasting — the culmination of devotion

🛒

What You Need

The Chhath Soop

Large bamboo winnowing basket — the primary vessel for offerings. Buy at least two.

From Market

  • Sugarcane stalks with leaves
  • Thekua (wheat flour cookies)
  • Banana bunches
  • Coconut, pomelo, pear, lemon
  • Raw turmeric and ginger root
  • Jaggery, wheat flour, ghee

Flowers & Puja Items

  • Marigold and loose petals
  • Brass/copper Kalash vessels
  • Diyas, incense, camphor
  • Kumkum, chandan, mauli thread

What to Wear

  • Women: New yellow/orange saree
  • Men: White/yellow dhoti-kurta
  • Important: No leather at ghat!

Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • Maintain complete purity for all 4 days
  • Sleep on the floor, no bed
  • Observe Nirjala fast completely
  • Carry Soop on your head to ghat
  • Stand in water for both Arghya offerings
  • Offer three Arghayas each time
  • Distribute prasad to all at ghat
  • Make Thekua at home
  • Sing Chhath Geet at the ghat
  • Break fast with prasad first

Don'ts

  • Don't wear leather at the ghat
  • Don't eat non-veg, onion, or garlic
  • Don't sleep on a bed during 4 days
  • Don't break Nirjala fast early
  • Don't use plastic or pollute ghat
  • Don't skip Sandhya Arghya
  • Don't treat it as a spectacle
  • Don't push or rush at crowded ghats

"Chhath Puja is the festival that chooses to worship the sun not at noon — not at its most glorious, most powerful, most obvious moment — but at sunset, when it is leaving. Standing in cold water, fasting for 36 hours, facing a sun that is disappearing — and saying: thank you. Even now. Especially now. That is not just devotion. That is wisdom."
