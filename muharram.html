📖 About

Muharram is the first month of the Islamic lunar calendar — making its first day the Islamic New Year. It commemorates the Hijra — Prophet Muhammad's (PBUH) migration from Mecca to Medina in 622 CE — the founding event from which all Islamic history is dated. The most significant day within it is Ashura — the 10th of Muharram — one of the holiest days in the Islamic calendar.

Muharram carries two deeply distinct layers of meaning. For all Muslims, it is the sacred new year and the day of Prophet Musa's (AS) salvation from Pharaoh. For Shia Muslims, it is the month of Azadari — mourning — for the martyrdom of Imam Husain (AS), grandson of the Prophet (PBUH), at the plains of Karbala in 680 CE. Both meanings are honored.

"The Prophet (PBUH) arrived in Medina and found the Jews fasting on Ashura. He asked why. They said: this is the day Allah saved Musa and his people from Pharaoh — so we fast in gratitude. The Prophet said: we have more right to Musa than you — and fasted, and encouraged all Muslims to fast on this day."

⭐ Significance

Muharram is the reminder that the most powerful thing a human being can do is stand for truth when standing costs everything. Imam Husain had every chance to compromise, to submit, to survive. He chose Karbala instead — dignity over survival. For Sunni Muslims, Ashura is a day of fasting and gratitude for divine rescue. For Shia Muslims it is the deepest grief in the calendar. Both are, at their core, about the same thing: a God who sees. A people who remember. A sacrifice that must never be forgotten.

🕌 Two Traditions — How Muslims Observe Muharram

☾ Sunni Observances

  • Fast on the 9th AND 10th of Muharram together — fasting only the 10th alone is discouraged
  • The Prophet (PBUH) said fasting on Ashura expiates the sins of the previous year
  • Increase voluntary prayers, Quran recitation, and dhikr throughout the month
  • Muharram is one of Islam's four sacred months — sin is especially prohibited
  • Give generously in charity — the month carries immense spiritual weight
  • No special processions or ceremonies — Sunni observance is primarily personal worship

🖤 Shia Observances — Azadari

  • Wear black clothes for the first 10 days as a sign of mourning
  • No weddings, music, or celebrations during the mourning period
  • Attend Majlis — nightly mourning gatherings at Imambargahs or homes
  • Recite Nauha — mournful elegies for the martyrs of Karbala
  • Perform Matam — rhythmic chest-beating as sincere expression of grief
  • Recite Ziyarat Ashura on the 10th — salutation to Imam Husain (AS)
  • Participate in the Ashura Juloos — the mourning procession
  • Set up Sabil stalls — distribute water and food freely to all

📜 The Story of Karbala — Understand It Fully

🖤 What Happened at Karbala — 680 CE

After the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) passed away, leadership eventually passed to Yazid ibn Muawiyah — a man known for corruption and oppressive rule. Yazid demanded that Imam Husain (AS) — the Prophet's own grandson — publicly pledge allegiance to legitimize his rule.

Imam Husain refused. He said: "A person like me cannot pledge allegiance to a person like him." He knew refusal meant death. He left Medina with his family — women, children, and 72 companions — heading toward Kufa in Iraq. At Karbala, Yazid's army of thousands surrounded them and cut off their access to the Euphrates river. For three days in the desert heat, Imam Husain's camp had no water. Children cried of thirst.

On the morning of Ashura, Imam Husain offered his companions the chance to leave — he turned off the lamps at night so they could slip away without shame. Every single one stayed.

In the battle that followed, all 72 companions fell — including his teenage son Ali Akbar, his nephew Qasim, and his infant son Ali Asghar, brought to the battlefield to beg for water and killed by an arrow. Imam Husain was the last to fall — exhausted, wounded, thirsty. He was beheaded. The women and children were taken captive to Damascus.

It was Sayyida Zainab (AS), Imam Husain's sister, who despite captivity and grief delivered one of the most powerful speeches in human history before Yazid's court — declaring that Husain's sacrifice would never be forgotten. She was right. 1,400 years later — it has not been.

"Death with dignity is better than life with humiliation." — Imam Husain (AS), before the battle of Karbala

🕯️ Ashura — The 10th of Muharram

🌙 Morning
Wake Early — Bathe & Pray

Wake before Fajr. Bathe. For Sunni Muslims: begin the two-day fast (9th and 10th). Spend the day in extra prayers, Quran recitation, and dhikr. For Shia Muslims: wear black, go to the Imambargah for morning prayers and recitation. The day is set apart from all other days — treat it as such from the first moment.

📿 Shia — Core Practice
Ziyarat Ashura

Recite Ziyarat Ashura — one of the most sacred Shia prayers — on this day. A direct salutation to Imam Husain (AS) and his companions, expressing love, grief, and solidarity with their sacrifice. It includes 100 salutations and is considered to carry immense spiritual merit when recited specifically on Ashura.

"Assalamu alayka ya Aba Abdillah, Assalamu alayka wa rahmatullahi wa barakatuh"

Peace be upon you O Aba Abdillah (Imam Husain), peace be upon you and Allah's mercy and blessings.
🖤 Every Evening — Days 1–10
Majlis — The Mourning Gathering

Every evening of the first 10 days, Majlis gatherings are held at Imambargahs, mosques, and homes. A Zakir recounts the events of Karbala in deeply moving detail — building to an intense climax of the martyrdom. The congregation responds with Nauha (mournful elegies) and Matam (rhythmic chest-beating). Anyone — Muslim or non-Muslim — may attend with respectful presence.

🚶 10th Muharram
Ashura Juloos — The Procession

The most visible public expression of Muharram — a massive mourning procession on the 10th. Led by Alams (battle standards on decorated horses), participants walk chanting "Ya Husain" with Matam throughout. A Taboot (symbolic coffin) is often carried. The procession concludes where final prayers are offered for the martyrs of Karbala.

"Ya Husain, Ya Husain" · "Labbaik Ya Husain" — We are here for you, O Husain · "Nara-e-Haideri — Ya Ali"

💧 Sabil — The Tradition of Free Water

One of the most beautiful Muharram traditions is Sabil — the free distribution of water, sherbat, and food to all passersby throughout the 10 days. This directly commemorates the three days Imam Husain's camp was denied access to the Euphrates river before the battle of Karbala. Every cup of water given freely on Muharram is a cup given in the name of those who died thirsty at Karbala.

💧 Every Cup is an Act of Solidarity
Shia families and communities set up Sabil stalls outside their homes and along procession routes — distributing cold water, rose sherbat, milk, and food to absolutely everyone who passes — regardless of religion, caste, or background. This is considered one of the highest acts of Muharram seva. Participating — either setting up a Sabil or simply accepting the water given with gratitude — is a deeply moving act of community on this day.

🍽️ Food of Muharram — Nazr

Muharram is a period of mourning — food is simple and minimal, never festive. The primary food tradition is Nazr — simple cooked food prepared with the intention of offering in memory of the martyrs, then distributed freely to all — neighbors, the poor, and passersby of any religion. Accepting Nazr food is considered a blessing.

🍲 Haleem
Slow-cooked meat and lentil stew — the most specifically associated Muharram Nazr food in Hyderabad and South Asian Shia communities. Cooked in large quantities and distributed freely.
🍛 Dal & Khichdi
Simple lentil and rice preparations — traditional Nazr food offered in memory of the martyrs. Simple and nourishing, befitting a period of mourning.
🍚 Biryani (Nazr)
In many communities, biryani is prepared as Nazr and distributed — especially at Imambargahs feeding hundreds throughout the 10 days.
💧 Sharbat & Water
Distributed at Sabil stalls — cold water, rose sherbat, milk — in direct memory of the thirst of Karbala.
🌙 Suhoor & Iftar
For those observing the Ashura fast — standard Islamic fasting rules apply. Eat at Suhoor before Fajr. Break at Maghrib with dates and water.

Dua on Ashura Fast — Sunni · Throughout the Fasting Day
"Allahumma inni as'aluka an tarzuqani tawbatan nasuha qabla al-mawt"
O Allah, I ask You to grant me sincere repentance before death. Entirely appropriate for the gravity of Ashura — the fast of this day expiates the sins of the previous year according to Hadith. Accompany the fast with this dua throughout the day alongside extra Quran recitation.
💧
The Chant of the Procession — Said with Full Voice Throughout the Juloos
"Ya Husain, Ya Husain" — "Labbaik Ya Husain" — "Nara-e-Haideri — Ya Ali"
O Husain, O Husain — We are here for you, O Husain — The call of Haider, O Ali. These chants fill the Ashura procession — said in unison by thousands, they become a declaration across 1,400 years: we remember. We have not forgotten. We are still here.
📿
Dhikr Throughout Muharram — All Muslims
"Subhanallah · Alhamdulillah · Allahu Akbar"
Glory be to Allah · All praise is to Allah · Allah is the Greatest. The foundational dhikr of Islam — repeated throughout this sacred month. Muharram is one of the four sacred months in Islam — the weight of the month calls for increased worship, Quran recitation, and this dhikr throughout every day.

📌 Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do's

  • Sunni: Fast on the 9th AND 10th of Muharram together
  • Shia: Wear black, attend Majlis every evening of the first 10 days
  • Recite Ziyarat Ashura on the 10th — with full attention to its meaning
  • Attend the Ashura procession with respect and sincerity
  • Set up or support a Sabil stall — distribute water and food freely to all
  • Give charity generously throughout Muharram
  • Distribute Nazr food to neighbors of all faiths
  • Read and understand the full story of Karbala — its lessons of standing against injustice are universal
  • If attending a Majlis as a visitor — sit quietly; silent respectful presence is entirely appropriate
  • Increase Quran recitation and personal worship throughout the month

✕ Don'ts

  • Do not hold weddings, celebrations, or joyful events in the first 10 days — this is a period of mourning
  • Do not fast only on the 10th alone (Sunni) — fast the 9th and 10th together
  • Do not photograph mourners or procession participants intrusively — the grief is sincere and sacred
  • Do not mock or trivialize Matam — it is a profound expression of genuine grief
  • Do not treat Muharram as merely a cultural event — understand Karbala and let it move you
  • Do not refuse Nazr food or Sabil water — accepting it respectfully honors the tradition
