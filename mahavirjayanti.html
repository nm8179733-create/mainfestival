📖 About

Mahavir Jayanti celebrates the birth of Lord Mahavira — the 24th and last Tirthankara of Jainism, born in Vaishali, Bihar around 599 BCE. He renounced his royal life at 30, meditated for 12.5 years, attained Kevala Jnana (omniscience), taught for 30 years, then attained Moksha at 72.

He is called Mahavira (Great Hero) for conquering his own senses and karma — not for military victory. His five core teachings: Ahimsa (non-violence), Satya (truth), Asteya (non-stealing), Brahmacharya (celibacy), and Aparigraha (non-possessiveness).

"In Jainism, Tirthankaras are not Gods who grant wishes — they have attained complete liberation. You worship Mahavira's qualities — ahimsa, equanimity, knowledge — so those qualities grow within you. Do not ask for personal favors. Pray to imbibe his qualities."

This is a day of quiet reflection, fasting, prayer, and renewing commitment to non-violent living — not a loud colorful festival.

⭐ Significance

In a world that celebrates conquest, Mahavira conquered himself. On this day we ask: how much of his courage do we have — to live the way he taught? Mahavir Jayanti is the quietest of India's great celebrations and perhaps the most radical. The five vows he lived — non-violence, truth, non-stealing, celibacy, non-possessiveness — are not rituals. They are a complete philosophy of liberation.

🎭 Activities & Ceremony

✦ Step 1
Dress in White & Remove Leather

White is Jainism's color of purity. Remove all leather items — no leather anywhere near the temple or puja space at any point.

✦ Step 2
Samayika — 48 Minutes of Stillness

Sit cross-legged, hands folded, eyes downcast. Withdraw from all worldly thoughts. Recite the Navkar Mantra slowly throughout.

"Namo Arihantanam, Namo Siddhanam…" — the most important practice of the day
✦ Step 3
Enter the Jain Temple

Remove shoes outside. Wash hands and feet. Cover head with white cloth. Enter quietly, bow with folded hands.

"Jai Jinendra" — Victory to the Conqueror of Self
✦ Step 4
Ashtaprakari Puja — Eight Offerings

Jal → Chandan → Pushpa → Dhoop → Deepa → Akshata → Naivedya → Phala. Each with its mantra: "Om Hreem Shri Mahaveera [offering] Puja"

✦ Step 5
Janma Abhishekam

Pour Panchamritam over the idol — water, milk, curd, ghee, sugar water, sandalwood water, saffron water — reenacting Indra's bathing of newborn Mahavira on Mount Meru.

✦ Step 6
Rath Yatra — The Procession

Walk alongside the decorated chariot carrying Mahavira's idol. Monks and nuns in white robes lead. Quiet, dignified, reverent — very different from loud festival processions.

✦ Step 7
Jeev Daya — Release Caged Birds

Buy and release caged birds or fish from markets. Compassion for living beings — the most meaningful charitable act of this day.

✦ Step 8
One Simple Meal Before Sunset

Plain dal, rice, above-ground vegetables, fruits, curd, dry fruits. No eating after sunset — a core Jain practice. No root vegetables in strict observance.

🙏 Key Prayers & Mantras

📿
Navkar Mantra — The Supreme Jain Prayer (recite 108 times)
"Namo Arihantanam — Namo Siddhanam
Namo Ayariyanam — Namo Uvajjhayanam
Namo Loe Savva Sahunam
Eso Pancha Namokkaro, Savva Pavappanasano
Mangalanam Cha Savvesim, Padhamam Havai Mangalam"
This fivefold salutation destroys all sins and is the most auspicious of all things. It bows to qualities and states of being — not specific Gods. Entirely universal.
🙏
Primary Greeting & Salutation
"Jai Jinendra"
Victory to the Conqueror of Self. Both the Jain greeting and salutation to the deity — use whenever entering a temple or meeting another Jain.
✨
Ten Supreme Virtues — Recite & Renew Your Commitment
"Uttam Kshama, Uttam Mardava, Uttam Arjava, Uttam Shaucha, Uttam Satya,
Uttam Sanyam, Uttam Tapa, Uttam Tyaga, Uttam Akinchanya, Uttam Brahmacharya"
Supreme Forgiveness, Humility, Straightforwardness, Purity, Truth, Self-restraint, Austerity, Renunciation, Non-possessiveness, Celibacy.

📌 Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do's

  • Wear white; remove all leather before puja
  • Do Samayika meditation — 48 minutes minimum
  • Perform Ashtaprakari Puja and Abhishekam
  • Chant Navkar Mantra 108 times throughout the day
  • Release caged birds or donate to a Panjrapole
  • Observe strict vegetarianism — no root vegetables if possible
  • Eat only one simple meal, before sunset
  • Visit the temple quietly and with full reverence

✕ Don'ts

  • No leather anywhere near the temple or puja
  • No non-vegetarian food — not even eggs
  • No root vegetables (onion, garlic, potato, carrot)
  • Do not ask Mahavira for personal favors
  • Do not be noisy — Jain worship is deeply inward
  • Do not eat after sunset
  • Do not wear colored clothes if avoidable
