Dussehra

Vijayadashami — The Tenth Day of Victory
📅 October 19, 2026 · 10th Ashwin Shukla Paksha

The day truth wins. Dharma conquers adharma. Rama defeats Ravana. Durga defeats Mahishasura. The most auspicious day in the Hindu calendar.

📖

About

Dussehra celebrates two simultaneous victories of good over evil. Lord Rama killed the ten-headed demon king Ravana on this day after a nine-day battle — rescuing Goddess Sita and destroying adharma. Simultaneously, Goddess Durga completed her nine-day battle and slew the buffalo demon Mahishasura on this tenth day. The name "Dussehra" comes from "Dasha-Hara" — the destruction of the ten heads of Ravana, representing the ten vices within each of us.

Both victories carry the same eternal message: dharma always ultimately defeats adharma. No matter how powerful evil becomes — Ravana was brilliant, powerful, and learned — truth endures. And on the tenth morning, it always stands.

How It's Celebrated Across India

North India

Massive Ramlila performances and Ravana Dahan — effigies up to 200 ft tall burned with firecrackers. Ramleela Maidan, Delhi hosts the grandest spectacle.

West Bengal

Durga Visarjan — the immersion of Durga idols with Sindoor Khela and emotional farewell processions to the Hooghly river.

Maharashtra

Apta leaf exchange as symbolic gold — "Sonachi Ahe, Sona Ghya." A uniquely beautiful tradition rooted in the story of Kautsa and King Raghu.

Karnataka / Tamil Nadu

Ayudha Puja — elaborate worship of vehicles, tools, and instruments. Mysore Dussehra is a UNESCO-recognized cultural heritage event.

Gujarat

Jalebi and Fafda for breakfast — a cultural institution. Then Garba and Dandiya Raas all night during the preceding Navratri.

Himachal Pradesh

Kullu Dussehra — a week-long fair where deities from 200+ temples are brought together in magnificent procession. Runs 7 days after Dussehra.

Rituals & Activities

🪔 Aparajita Puja

The closing ritual of Navratri — performed at the northeast corner of the home or at a Shami tree. Offer kumkum, marigold, and chant "Om Aparajitayai Namaha" 108 times.

🌿 Shami Tree Worship

The Shami tree is the sacred center of Vijayadashami. The Pandavas hid their weapons here; Rama worshipped it before battle. Performing puja here destroys accumulated sin.

⚔️ Ayudha Puja

Worship your tools, vehicles, instruments, and professional equipment. Clean them, apply kumkum and turmeric, offer flowers, and pray that they serve you with integrity all year.

🔥 Ravana Dahan

Watch effigies of Ravana, Kumbhakarna, and Meghnad burn. More than a spectacle — stand before the fire and choose which inner vice you are consciously burning today.

🎭 Ramlila

Nine-day dramatic enactment of the Ramayana culminating on Dussehra. Ramnagar, Varanasi hosts the most traditional — spread over 4 km, performed for 200+ years.

🏹 Begin New Endeavors

Vijayadashami is the single most auspicious day to start anything new. Do one concrete first step at the Vijay Muhurta — the energy of victory carries your beginning forward.

Key Mantras & Prayers

Aparajita Puja Mantra

"Om Aparajitayai Namaha"
Chant 108 times at the Shami tree or northeast corner of your home.

Shami Tree Prayer

"Shami Shamayate Papam, Shami Shatro Vinashinee
Arjunasya Dhanur Dhaarini, Raamasya Priya Darshini
Tava Patra Grahanena Mama Paapam Vinashyatu"
Shami destroys sin, Shami destroys enemies. She held Arjuna's bow, she is beloved by Rama. May my sins be destroyed by taking your leaves.

Prayer During Ravana Dahan

"Om Asato Ma Sad Gamaya
Tamaso Ma Jyotir Gamaya
Mrityor Ma Amritam Gamaya
Om Shanti Shanti Shantihi"
Lead me from untruth to truth. Lead me from darkness to light. Lead me from death to immortality. Om peace peace peace.

Economy & Scale

Market Impact

The festival generates ₹10,000+ crore through firecrackers, traditional clothing, sweets, idol-making, and decorative materials.

Employment

Seasonal employment for thousands — effigy makers, firework manufacturers, artisans, performers, and event organizers.

Tourism

Mysore Dussehra and Ramnagar Ramlila draw hundreds of thousands of domestic and international tourists every year.

Cultural Heritage

Ramnagar Ramlila is on UNESCO's Intangible Cultural Heritage list. Mysore Dussehra has been celebrated continuously for over 400 years.

Traditional Foods to Eat

Every region celebrates Dussehra with its own signature food. Some are sacred prasad, some mark the breaking of the Navratri fast — all are deeply tied to the festival's spirit of abundance and victory.

Gujarat
🍯
Jalebi & Fafda

The iconic Dussehra breakfast in Gujarat — crispy chickpea flour fafda dunked alongside spirals of syrupy hot jalebi. So specific to this day it has become a cultural institution.

Pan-India
🍚
Kheer (Rice Pudding)

Creamy, slow-cooked rice pudding with saffron, cardamom, and nuts — offered as prasad and eaten to break the Navratri fast. The sweetness symbolises the sweetness of victory.

North India
🫓
Halwa-Puri & Chana

The beloved North Indian fast-breaking feast — fluffy deep-fried puris with sooji halwa and spiced chickpeas. Eaten after Navratri Ashtami or Navami kanya puja, carried into Dussehra feasting.

South India
🥤
Panakam

A traditional temple drink of jaggery, water, cardamom, ginger, and black pepper — offered as prasad at Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu temples on Vijayadashami. Cooling, fragrant, sacred.

Maharashtra
🍬
Apta Leaf Sweets

In Maharashtra, sweets are distributed alongside the Apta leaf exchange — mithai boxes passed between families, neighbours, and friends as a gesture of sharing abundance and goodwill.

West Bengal
🧁
Sandesh & Mishti Doi

Bengal's Durga Visarjan on Dussehra is followed by an elaborate feast — fresh chenna Sandesh and thick sweet Mishti Doi (yogurt) are non-negotiable parts of the farewell celebration.

Do's & Don'ts

Dussehra is not just a public spectacle — it is a personal spiritual ceremony. How you observe it determines what you take from it.

✅ Do's — Observe These
✓
Perform Aparajita Puja firstAlways begin the day with Aparajita Puja at the northeast corner of your home or at a Shami tree — this properly closes Navratri.
✓
Mark the Vijay MuhurtaCheck drikpanchang.com for the exact window. Do at least one new, meaningful action during this time — it is the single most auspicious slot in the entire Hindu year.
✓
Worship the Shami treePour water at its roots, apply kumkum, offer marigolds, chant the Shami prayer. Collect a few leaves to take home as a blessed offering.
✓
Do Ayudha Puja properlyClean your vehicle, laptop, instruments, or tools before the puja — do not worship them in a dirty or disorganised state. The cleaning is part of the ritual.
✓
Attend Ravana Dahan consciouslyStand before the burning effigy and consciously name which inner vice — anger, ego, jealousy — you are choosing to work on this year. Make it a personal commitment, not a spectacle.
✓
Exchange Shami / Apta leavesIn Maharashtra and South India, exchange Shami or Apta leaves with family, neighbours, and friends as symbolic gold — carry the spirit of generosity forward.
✓
Wear bright auspicious coloursSaffron, red, yellow, or orange — traditional dress is preferred. The brightness of your clothing matches the brightness of the day's energy.
✓
Visit a Rama or Durga templeSeek special Vijayadashami darshan — both Rama temples and Durga temples are equally appropriate on this day for prayers and blessings.
✓
Break the Navratri fast joyfullyIf you fasted for 9 days, Dussehra is the day to celebrate with a full traditional meal — the fast-breaking is itself part of the victory celebration.
✓
Begin something new todayStart the first line of a new project, sign a document, strike a first note, take a first step — even small beginnings made on Dussehra carry forward with the day's victorious energy.
🚫 Don'ts — Avoid These
✗
Don't miss the Vijay MuhurtaThis is the single most critical observance of the day. Missing it entirely — sleeping through or ignoring it — wastes the most auspicious window in the entire year.
✗
Don't watch Ravana Dahan passivelyAttending only for the fireworks spectacle, without any inner engagement, misses the entire spiritual purpose of the ceremony. It is an inner ritual as much as an outer one.
✗
Don't skip Aparajita PujaStarting Dussehra without this morning puja leaves Navratri improperly closed. It is the formal conclusion of 10 days of spiritual work — don't neglect it.
✗
Don't do Ayudha Puja carelesslyWorshipping a dirty car or a dusty instrument defeats the purpose. The act of thorough cleaning before the puja is non-negotiable — it signals respect and intentionality.
✗
Don't treat Dussehra as just a holidayIt is the culmination of 10 days of inner work done during Navratri. Treating it as a day off without any observance breaks the spiritual continuity of the entire period.
✗
Don't harm or cut the Shami treeThe Shami tree is sacred. Take only a few leaves with reverence — never break branches or damage the tree in any way during your worship.
✗
Don't use Ayudha Puja weapons carelesslyWorshipping weapons or sharp tools also means committing to use them with care and integrity. Don't misuse tools that have been blessed on this day.
✗
Don't delay starting new projectsProcrastinating specifically past the Vijay Muhurta on this day means postponing until the next Vijayadashami — a full year away. Begin now, however small the first step.
✗
Don't quarrel or engage in conflictDussehra is a day of dharma's triumph — picking fights, harsh words, or disputes on this day is deeply inauspicious and goes against the spirit of the occasion.
✗
Don't consume alcohol or non-veg (during puja)Especially if performing formal puja rituals during the Vijay Muhurta — remain sattvic, clean, and clear-headed throughout the morning and afternoon observances.
