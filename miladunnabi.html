📖 About

Milad-un-Nabi — also called Mawlid — commemorates the birth of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ born on 12th Rabi ul-Awwal, 570 CE in Mecca, Arabia. He is the final Prophet and Messenger of Allah — the Seal of the Prophets (Khatam-un-Nabiyyin).

He was born an orphan — his father died before his birth, his mother when he was six. At age 40 he received the first revelation of the Quran in the Cave of Hira. For 23 years he conveyed Allah's message — facing persecution, exile, and war — until Islam was established across Arabia. He passed away at age 63 in Medina.

"Wa maa arsalnaaka illaa rahmatan lil-'aalameen"
"And We have not sent you except as a mercy to all the worlds."
— Surah Al-Anbiya 21:107

A note on observance: Milad-un-Nabi is observed differently across Muslim communities. Sunni Muslims — particularly in South Asia, West Africa, and the Arab world — celebrate with processions, gatherings, and poetry. Some Sunni scholars and Salafi/Wahhabi Muslims consider public celebration of Mawlid an innovation (Bid'ah) and do not celebrate it. Shia Muslims observe it as a day of joy. Both positions are held sincerely — this guide presents the celebratory tradition while respecting all viewpoints.

🌿 Who Was He — The Prophet's ﷺ Character

Before observing Milad-un-Nabi meaningfully, knowing who the Prophet ﷺ was is essential — because the entire observance is about loving and following him.

✦ Al-Amin
The Trustworthy One

Even before prophethood, Muhammad ﷺ was known throughout Mecca as the most trustworthy and honest person. His enemies — who fought him bitterly — never accused him of dishonesty. He was the standard of human character in a society of merchants where trust was currency.

✦ His Mercy
Champion of the Weak

He was the first person in 7th century Arabia to speak for the rights of women, orphans, slaves, and the poor. He abolished the practice of burying infant girls alive. He said: "The best of you are those who are best to their families." His social revolution was as radical as his theological one.

✦ His Humility
Prophet Who Mended His Own Shoes

Despite being the Prophet of God, he mended his own shoes, sewed his own clothes, helped with household work, and never considered himself above any person. He sat on the ground with the poor. He said: "I eat as a slave eats and I sit as a slave sits."

✦ His Forgiveness
The General Amnesty of Mecca

When he returned to Mecca after years of persecution — with a full army capable of revenge — he declared a general amnesty for all his enemies. He said simply: "Go — you are all free." This act of forgiveness, with full power to do otherwise, defines his character more than any other single moment.

✦ His Love
His Last Words

His last words on his deathbed were for his Ummah (community): "My Ummah, my Ummah." Not for power, not for legacy, not for himself — for his people. Celebrating his birthday is the community's expression of that love returned, across fourteen centuries.

⭐ Significance

Milad-un-Nabi is the day we remember that God's greatest gift to humanity was not a scripture or a law — it was a person. A man who showed by the way he walked, spoke, ate, laughed, grieved, forgave, and loved — exactly what it looks like when a human being lives completely for God and completely for others. The best way to celebrate his birthday is to try, for one day, to live the way he lived.

🌅 How to Observe the Day

✦ Morning
Rise, Bathe, Wear Green, Apply Attar

Wake up early. Take a full bath. Wear green clothes — green was the Prophet's ﷺ favorite color and the color most associated with him. Apply attar (non-alcoholic perfume) — applying fragrance is one of the Prophet's most emphasized Sunnahs. He loved fragrance and said it was among the things of this world made beloved to him.

✦ Morning — Most Important
Durood Ibrahim — 100 Times Minimum

Begin the day with Durood Ibrahim — the salutation upon the Prophet ﷺ recited in every prayer. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever sends one salutation upon me, Allah sends ten salutations upon him." On Milad-un-Nabi, recite it at least 100 times. Many devotees recite it 1000 times or continuously throughout the day.

"Allahumma Salli ala Muhammadin wa ala ali Muhammadin, Kama Sallayta ala Ibrahima wa ala ali Ibrahim — Innaka Hamidun Majid"
✦ Day
Milad Gathering at the Mosque or Dargah

Attend the central Milad gathering at your mosque, dargah, or community hall. The gathering includes: Quran recitation (Surah Al-Fatiha, Surah Ya-Sin) · collective Durood and Salawat in unison · Naat Sharif recitation · Seerah recitation by a scholar · Qiyam (standing at the moment of birth narration) · communal Dua with hands raised. The sound of hundreds of voices sending blessings upon the Prophet simultaneously is one of the most moving collective experiences in Islamic life.

✦ Day
Naat Sharif — Poetry in Praise

Naat is devotional poetry in praise of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ — in Urdu, Arabic, Persian. The most universally beloved is Burdah Sharif by 13th century Egyptian poet Imam Busiri — 162 verses of breathtaking praise in Arabic. Also essential: Tala al Badru Alayna — the song Medina sang when the Prophet arrived during the Hijra — the oldest recorded nasheed in history, sung at every Milad gathering. Listen on YouTube with translation if you don't know Arabic.

"Tala al badru alayna, min thaniyyatil wada — Wajaba shukru alayna, ma daa lillahi daa"
✦ Day
Read the Seerah — 30–60 Minutes Minimum

The single most meaningful act of Milad-un-Nabi — beyond all celebration — is reading and understanding the Prophet's life. Three essential books: The Sealed Nectar (Ar-Raheeq Al-Makhtum) by Mubarakpuri — most widely read Seerah, free online. Muhammad: His Life Based on the Earliest Sources by Martin Lings — most beautifully written English Seerah. In the Footsteps of the Prophet by Tariq Ramadan — modern, accessible, focused on character.

✦ All Day
Jaloos — The Milad Procession

In many Indian cities — Hyderabad, Mumbai, Lucknow, Delhi, and across South India — massive Jaloos (processions) fill the streets on Milad-un-Nabi. Green flags, banners with the Prophet's names, thousands chanting Durood and Naats. The entire month of Rabi ul-Awwal sees green lights and decorations on mosques and dargahs. If a Jaloos passes near you — stand respectfully and bow your head as it passes.

🌟 Following the Sunnah — Living the Festival

The most complete way to honor Milad-un-Nabi is to consciously follow the Prophet's Sunnah (his way of life) on this day. Each of these acts is directly from his practice — documented in authenticated Hadith.

😊 Smile at Everyone
"Your smiling at your brother is an act of charity." — Prophet Muhammad ﷺ
🍽️ Feed Someone
"Feed the hungry, visit the sick, and free the captive." — The Prophet ﷺ on three acts of charity
🗣️ Speak Gently
He never spoke harshly. Today — watch every word. Speak only what is true, kind, and necessary.
🤝 Visit the Sick
Among the most emphasized acts of his Sunnah — check on anyone who is unwell, even with a phone call.
💰 Give in Charity
"He was more generous than the blowing wind." — described in Hadith. Give something, however small.
🌿 Apply Attar
Fragrance was among "the things of this world made beloved to me" — the Prophet ﷺ. Use non-alcoholic attar today.
🌴 Eat Dates
He ate dates regularly and recommended them. Eat an odd number of dates today — 1, 3, 5, or 7.
🪥 Use Miswak
The Prophet's tooth-cleaning stick. "Were it not a hardship I would order it before every prayer." Using it today is direct Sunnah.
👁️ Lower Your Gaze
He was described as the most modest and gentle of people in his manner and gaze. Guard your character consciously today.

🗺️ How It Is Celebrated

Hyderabad
Grand Jaloos — Largest in India

Hyderabad's Milad-un-Nabi Jaloos — the Shaan-e-Rehmatul-lil-Alameen procession — is one of the largest in India with hundreds of thousands of participants moving through the old city. The entire city illuminates in green for the month of Rabi ul-Awwal.

Lucknow & Deoband
Scholarly Tradition

Lucknow's grand Milad mahfils (gatherings) are known for their elaborate Naat Sharif recitation and scholarly Seerah recitations. Deoband — home to one of Islam's most influential seminaries — reflects the more austere tradition that focuses on Seerah study over public processions.

Mumbai & Kerala
Malabar's Duff Muttu

Kerala's Muslim community celebrates with Duff Muttu — traditional drum-based devotional performances with Naat singing. Mumbai's Muslim neighborhoods light up with green decorations and large community gatherings at mosques and dargahs.

Dargahs Across India
Sufi Heart of Milad

At dargahs — the shrines of Sufi saints — Milad-un-Nabi is the grandest occasion of the year. Qawwali (devotional music) in praise of the Prophet ﷺ fills the night at dargahs from Ajmer to Delhi. The Sufi tradition holds the Prophet as the ultimate spiritual ideal — their Milad is an immersion in that love.

Global Milad
From Marrakech to Malacca

Milad-un-Nabi is celebrated by over a billion Muslims across the world. Morocco's national Mawlid is declared a public holiday with royal processions. Egypt's Al-Azhar mosque holds the most learned gathering. West Africa's Mouride Brotherhood holds an extraordinary annual gathering. The Prophet ﷺ belongs to all of humanity.

🙏 Key Prayers & Recitations

💚
Durood Ibrahim — Recite 100 Times Minimum (The Most Important Practice)
"Allahumma Salli ala Muhammadin wa ala ali Muhammadin — Kama Sallayta ala Ibrahima wa ala ali Ibrahim — Innaka Hamidun Majid — Allahumma Barik ala Muhammadin wa ala ali Muhammadin — Kama Barakta ala Ibrahima wa ala ali Ibrahim — Innaka Hamidun Majid"
O Allah, send blessings upon Muhammad and upon the family of Muhammad, as You sent blessings upon Ibrahim and the family of Ibrahim. Indeed You are Praiseworthy and Glorious. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever sends one salutation upon me, Allah sends ten salutations upon him." On Milad-un-Nabi, 100 times minimum — more is always more.
🌙
Simple Durood — Said Every Time His Name Is Mentioned
"Sallallahu Alayhi Wasallam"
May Allah's peace and blessings be upon him. This short salutation is obligatory for every Muslim every time the Prophet's name is mentioned. On Milad-un-Nabi — his name is mentioned thousands of times. Every single mention: Sallallahu Alayhi Wasallam. Let it become automatic.
✨
Surah Al-Ahzab 33:56 — Allah's Own Salutation Upon the Prophet
"Innallaha wa Malaikatahu Yusalluna alan Nabi — Ya ayyuhal lazeena amanu sallu alayhi wa sallimu tasleema"
Indeed Allah and His angels send blessings upon the Prophet. O you who believe — send blessings upon him and salute him with worthy salutation. This is the only verse in the Quran where Allah speaks of doing something alongside the angels — and it is sending blessings upon the Prophet ﷺ. When Allah and the angels do it, how much more should we.
🤲
Dua for the Prophet's Way — Say at the Close of the Day
"Allahumma Ahyini ala Sunnati Nabiyyika wa Tawaffani ala Millatihi"
O Allah, let me live according to the Sunnah of Your Prophet and let me die upon his way. The most complete Milad-un-Nabi prayer — asking not for wealth or ease but for the greatest possible gift: to live and die in alignment with the man whose birthday we celebrate.

📌 Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do's

  • Wear green — the Prophet's ﷺ color
  • Apply attar — his most loved Sunnah
  • Recite Durood Ibrahim 100 times minimum — the most important practice
  • Attend a Milad gathering at your mosque or dargah
  • Listen to Naat Sharif — especially Burdah Sharif and Tala al Badru Alayna
  • Read the Seerah — at least 30–60 minutes today
  • Feed others generously — neighbors, the poor, anyone near you
  • Consciously observe the nine Sunnah acts throughout the day
  • Distribute dates and sweets to those around you
  • Say Sallallahu Alayhi Wasallam every time his name is mentioned — make it automatic

✕ Don'ts

  • Do not celebrate in ways the Prophet ﷺ himself would not approve of — the best way to honor him is to follow him
  • Do not treat this as merely a cultural holiday — it is a day of love, learning, and living his way
  • Do not skip the Durood — it is the most direct spiritual connection to the Prophet ﷺ on this day
  • Do not waste food prepared for distribution — this is generosity, not display

He mended his own shoes. He sat on the ground. He forgave the people who tortured him. He spent his last breath saying his community's name. The greatest way to celebrate his birthday is to spend one day trying to be even slightly more like him.
