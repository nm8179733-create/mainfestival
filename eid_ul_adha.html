📖 About

Eid ul-Adha — "Festival of Sacrifice" — falls on the 10th of Dhul Hijjah, the final month of the Islamic lunar calendar. It commemorates one of the most profound acts of faith in human history: Prophet Ibrahim (AS) received a command from Allah in a dream to sacrifice his beloved son Ismail (AS). Without hesitation, both father and son submitted completely to Allah's will. As Ibrahim raised the knife, Allah stopped him — replacing Ismail with a ram.

This was not a test of cruelty but of complete surrender (Islam) to the divine will. Allah declared that He never wanted the sacrifice of the son — He wanted the sacrifice of the ego, the attachment, the love of anything above God. This day also coincides with the climax of Hajj — the annual pilgrimage to Mecca, one of Islam's Five Pillars.

"Eid ul-Adha asks every Muslim the hardest question faith can ask: what do you love most? And are you willing to place even that before God? Ibrahim answered with a knife raised over his son. Allah answered with a ram and the words: that is enough. Your surrender is complete."

⭐ Significance

Eid ul-Adha is not merely a ritual of sacrifice — it is a profound act of community justice. The Qurbani meat must be distributed: one third to the poor, one third to relatives, one third to the family. On this day, the poorest people eat meat. Eid ul-Adha is specifically designed so that no one goes hungry. It transforms a personal act of faith into a collective act of care. Ibrahim's surrender is meaningless unless it translates into your generosity toward others. That is the full meaning of this day.

🌅 Eid Morning — Step by Step

✦ First
Ghusl & Dressing

Take a full body bath (Ghusl). Wear new or best clothes — traditional white for men. Apply attar (alcohol-free perfume). Do not eat anything before the Eid prayer — this is specifically the opposite of Eid ul-Fitr and is Sunnah. The fast begins with Qurbani, not with Fajr.

✦ Always
Recite the Eid Takbeer

From the eve of Eid until after the Eid prayer, recite the Takbeer continuously — while walking, at home, on the way to the mosque. This is one of the most spiritually charged acts of the entire day.

"Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, La ilaha illallah, Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, Wa lillahil hamd" Allah is Greatest, Allah is Greatest, there is no god but Allah, Allah is Greatest, Allah is Greatest, and all praise belongs to Allah.
✦ Morning
Eid Prayer at Mosque or Eidgah

The Eid prayer is 2 Rakats with additional Takbeers — 7 in the first Rakat, 5 in the second. Simply follow the Imam. After prayer, the Imam delivers the Khutbah (sermon) — listen attentively. It covers the story of Ibrahim and Ismail and the meaning of Qurbani.

"Taqabbal Allahu minna wa minkum" May Allah accept from us and from you. — The Eid greeting after prayer.
✦ After Prayer
Qurbani — The Sacrifice

Qurbani begins only after the Eid prayer is complete. The animal faces Qibla. The person says "Bismillahi Allahu Akbar" and cuts the throat swiftly in one clean motion, minimizing suffering. The earlier after the prayer, the better.

✦ After Qurbani
Distribute the Meat — First to the Poor

Divide the meat into three equal parts. In many communities, the poor portion is given out first — before the family even eats. Do not keep all the meat. The distribution is not optional — it is the entire point of the day.

✦ Day
Visit Family, Feed Guests

After Qurbani, the family sits for the first meal. Traditionally the liver (kaleji) is cooked and eaten first. Visit parents and grandparents first. Give Eidi to children. Visit the graves of loved ones — recite Surah Al-Fatiha. Keep your home open — every visitor is fed.

🐑 Qurbani — The Sacrifice in Detail

Qurbani is Wajib (obligatory) for every adult Muslim who possesses wealth above the Nisab threshold. If you cannot perform it yourself, you can authorize a butcher or donate through an Islamic charity like Islamic Relief India — they perform Qurbani on your behalf and distribute the meat to the needy.

The animal: Goat or sheep (minimum 1 year) counts for one person. Cow or camel counts for up to seven people — seven families can share one cow. The animal must be healthy, free of defects, and above minimum age. Purchase in advance from livestock markets available in every city in the days before Eid.

Time window: Begins after Eid prayer on the 10th of Dhul Hijjah and can be performed until sunset of the 13th — four days total.

🍽️ Traditional Eid ul-Adha Foods

🫀
Kaleji (Liver Fry)
The liver of the sacrificed animal fried with onions, spices, and green chili — traditionally the very first dish cooked and eaten on Eid morning after Qurbani.
🍛
Mutton Biryani
Fragrant rice layered with fresh mutton from the Qurbani — the centerpiece of the Eid feast and an expression of the day's abundance and generosity.
🥩
Nihari & Korma
Slow-cooked rich meat gravies — nihari simmered overnight, korma slow-braised in yogurt and spices. The slow cooking honors the depth of the day.
🍢
Seekh & Shami Kebabs
Minced meat kebabs seasoned with whole spices and grilled over coals or pan-fried — a beloved Eid snack shared with visitors throughout the day.
🍜
Haleem
Slow-cooked meat and lentil stew — beloved Eid ul-Adha specialty across Hyderabad and Deccan communities. Requires hours of cooking and communal effort.
🍮
Sheer Khurma
Sweet vermicelli cooked in milk with dates, saffron, and dry fruits. Served to every visitor throughout the day — the sweet gesture of Eid hospitality.

🕋 The Hajj Connection

🕋

2 Million Muslims — One Moment

While most Muslims celebrate Eid ul-Adha at home, approximately 2 million Muslims from across the world are simultaneously performing Hajj in Mecca — the fifth Pillar of Islam. On the day of Eid, Hajj pilgrims stand at Muzdalifah and Mina, perform their Qurbani, shave their heads, and complete the remaining Hajj rituals.

On Eid ul-Adha, remember those performing Hajj in your prayers. And if Hajj is financially and physically possible for you, it is an obligation to perform it once in your lifetime. Every Muslim worldwide performing Qurbani today is spiritually connected to those 2 million pilgrims at Mina.

📌 Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do's

  • Take Ghusl and wear new or best clothes
  • Recite Takbeer continuously from the eve of Eid until after prayer
  • Do NOT eat before Eid prayer — opposite of Eid ul-Fitr
  • Attend Eid prayer in congregation at mosque or Eidgah
  • Perform Qurbani after prayer — within the four-day window
  • Distribute one third of meat to the poor — give this out first
  • Visit parents, grandparents, and elders first
  • Give Eidi to children; feed every visitor who comes to your home
  • Visit graves of loved ones and recite Surah Al-Fatiha
  • If unable to do Qurbani yourself, donate through Islamic Relief India

✕ Don'ts

  • Do not eat before Eid prayer — this is Sunnah and mandatory
  • Do not perform Qurbani before the Eid prayer is completed
  • Do not keep all the meat for yourself — distribution to the poor is obligatory
  • Do not waste any part of the sacrificed animal
  • Do not purchase a sick, defective, or underage animal for Qurbani
  • Do not treat Qurbani as merely a ritual — reflect on what Ibrahim surrendered and what you are willing to surrender
  • Do not forget that this day is equally about the 2 million pilgrims at Mina — remember them in your prayers
