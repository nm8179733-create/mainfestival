📖 About

Tulsi Vivah is the ceremonial wedding of Tulsi (the Holy Basil plant) with Lord Vishnu (in his Shaligram or Krishna form) — performed with all the rituals of a traditional Hindu wedding. It marks the end of Chaturmas — the four sacred monsoon months during which auspicious activities like marriages are paused. From Tulsi Vivah onward, the wedding season begins.

The Story — Vrinda and Jalandhar: A devoted woman named Vrinda was married to the demon king Jalandhar. Her absolute fidelity made him invincible — no God could defeat him. When Vishnu — through divine illusion — broke Vrinda's vow, Jalandhar fell. Devastated and enraged, Vrinda cursed Vishnu to become stone — he became Shaligram. Then she immolated herself.

"Vrinda's soul lives in this plant. She is now Tulsi — and I will marry her every year in the month of Kartika. Wherever Tulsi grows, I am present." — Lord Vishnu's declaration

Tulsi Vivah is Vishnu's eternal honoring of Vrinda's devotion — his acknowledgment of wrongdoing and his commitment to her. It is also the story of how the most sacred plant in Hinduism became sacred — through the purity and suffering of a devoted woman.

⭐ Significance

Tulsi Vivah is the festival where a plant becomes a bride — and in doing so, reminds us that the divine is not distant and untouchable. It grows in a clay pot on your terrace. It survived the night's cold. You watered it this morning without knowing you were tending to God's beloved. Today you dress her with a tiny bindi and a piece of red cloth — and the universe witnesses a wedding.

🗺️ Regions

Maharashtra
Tulsi Vivah

The most elaborate regional tradition — women dress the Tulsi as a full bride with jewelry, saree pieces, and bindi. Neighborhood women gather to sing wedding songs. The celebration continues for two days.

North India
Tulsi Vivah / Devuthani Ekadashi

Celebrated as Devuthani Ekadashi — "the Ekadashi when the Gods awaken." Full wedding ritual with sugarcane mandap. Kheer and sweets distributed. The wedding season officially begins.

Gujarat
Tulsi Vivah

Community celebrations at temples with elaborate decorations. Women perform the full Vivah puja together. Amla, sugarcane, and coconut prominently offered and distributed as prasad.

Rajasthan
Tulsi Vivah

Traditional communities observe with great devotion — the Tulsi plant dressed with real gold jewelry borrowed for the occasion. The entire neighborhood participates as wedding guests.

South India
Tulasi Kalyanam

Celebrated as Tulasi Kalyanam — the wedding of Tulasi and Vishnu in Vaishnava temples. The temple Tulasi plant is decorated and the wedding ceremony performed by priests with full Vedic rites.

🎭 The Wedding Ceremony — 10 Steps

✦ Morning
Dress the Tulsi Bride

Apply Haldi (turmeric) to the Tulsi pot — as done to a human bride. Drape a small red or yellow cloth as her saree. Apply kumkum bindi at the top of the central stem. Place tiny bangles and jewelry around the plant. Garland with marigold and jasmine.

✦ Morning
Build the Sugarcane Mandap

Stand four sugarcane stalks upright at each corner of the Tulsi pot. Tie together at the top with mauli thread to form a canopy. Hang small marigold garlands between stalks. Place the Shaligram / Vishnu idol beside the Tulsi — this is the groom. Dress him with a tiny yellow cloth and chandan tilak.

✦ Evening
Light Diyas & Begin Puja

Perform at Pradosh Kaal — after sunset, in the twilight. Light all diyas around the mandap. Light incense. Begin with Ganesh puja: "Om Gam Ganapataye Namaha" — 11 times.

✦ Step 4
Kalash Sthapana

Place a Kalash (brass pot) filled with water, Gangajal, and mango leaves, topped with a coconut. This Kalash represents divine presence at the wedding — as it would at any Hindu wedding ceremony.

✦ Step 5
Tulsi Puja — Worship the Bride

Apply fresh turmeric and kumkum to the Tulsi pot. Offer fresh flowers. Sprinkle akshat. Apply chandan to the Shaligram.

"Om Tulasyai Vidmahe, Vishnu Priyayai Dheemahi, Tanno Brindah Prachodayat"
✦ Step 6
Jaimala & Kanya Daan

Exchange garlands between Tulsi and Shaligram — the divine Jaimala. Then perform Kanya Daan: pour water from a vessel over both hands placed above the Tulsi, symbolically giving Vrinda to Vishnu as bride.

"Imam Tulaseem Devi Mahyam Dataam, Shri Vishnu Paramatmane, Gruhanami Vidhi Drishtena, Supreeta Bhava Sarvada"
✦ Step 7
Mangalsutra & Saptapadi

Tie a red mauli thread between Tulsi and Shaligram — the sacred binding thread. Then carry the Shaligram around the Tulsi plant seven times, chanting "Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya" once per circle. Pray for one blessing with each of the seven rounds.

✦ Step 8
Panchamritam Abhishekam

Pour Panchamritam over the Shaligram — milk, curd, ghee, honey, sugar — while chanting "Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya." Then pour Gangajal to cleanse. Sprinkle a few drops of panchamritam and Gangajal on the Tulsi plant.

✦ Step 9
Amla Offering & Aarti

Offer fresh amla — the most important specific offering of Tulsi Vivah, sacred to both Tulsi and Vishnu. Also offer sugarcane, coconut, banana. Then wave camphor clockwise before the mandap while singing the Tulsi Aarti.

✦ Step 10
Pradakshina & Wedding Feast

Walk around the entire mandap seven times as a family — joyful and together. Bow forehead to ground before Tulsi and Shaligram. Then distribute kheer, sweets, and fruits to all neighbors as wedding prasad. Women sing traditional wedding songs.

🌙 Daily Tulsi Worship — Kartika Month

Tulsi Vivah falls at the end of Kartika month — the entire month is dedicated to Tulsi worship. The most powerful daily practice is simple:

Every evening of Kartika: Light a ghee diya before the Tulsi plant at sunset — called Tulsi Deepa. Walk around the plant three times clockwise. Fold hands and chant:

"Yani Kani Cha Papani, Brahma Hatyadikani Cha — Tani Tani Pranashyanti, Pradakshina Pade Pade" — All sins are destroyed with each step of circumambulation around Tulsi.

The Padma Purana says lighting a diya before Tulsi in Kartika earns merit equivalent to giving a thousand cows in charity. This daily evening circumambulation with a lit diya — just two minutes, every sunset — is one of the simplest and most powerful devotional practices in all of Hinduism.

🙏 Key Prayers & Mantras

🌿
Primary Tulsi Mantra — 108 Times
"Om Tulasyai Namaha"
I bow to Tulsi. The simplest and most direct prayer — chant 108 times in the morning before the ceremony. Each repetition is an acknowledgment that this plant is not ordinary.
✨
Tulsi Gayatri — Chanted During Puja
"Om Tulasyai Vidmahe, Vishnu Priyayai Dheemahi
Tanno Brindah Prachodayat"
We meditate on Tulsi — beloved of Vishnu — may Vrinda illuminate us. The Gayatri form of the Tulsi mantra, carrying all the resonance of her identity as Vrinda, as Vishnu's beloved, as purifier.
💛
Kanya Daan Prayer — Giving the Bride
"Imam Tulaseem Devi Mahyam Dataam, Shri Vishnu Paramatmane
Gruhanami Vidhi Drishtena, Supreeta Bhava Sarvada"
I offer this Goddess Tulsi to Lord Vishnu — may he always be pleased with this offering. Spoken as water flows from the vessel — symbolically releasing the bride into her divine marriage.
🪔
Tulsi Stotram — Daily Prayer
"Vrindavani Vrinda Vishwa Pavani
Pushpasara Nandini Tulsi Krishna Jeevani
Vishnu Priya Namostute"
O Vrindavani, Vrinda, purifier of the world, essence of flowers, delight of Nandini, Tulsi, life of Krishna — beloved of Vishnu — I bow to you.

📌 Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do's

  • Dress the Tulsi plant with full love — she is the bride, treat her as one
  • Build the sugarcane mandap — the most distinctive visual of Tulsi Vivah
  • Dress Tulsi in a tiny saree, bindi, and jewelry — this dressing is one of the most joyful parts
  • Perform the full Vivah: Jaimala, Kanya Daan, and Saptapadi
  • Light maximum diyas around the mandap — the more light, the more auspicious
  • Distribute kheer and sweets to all neighbors as wedding prasad
  • Offer amla specifically — the most important offering of this day
  • Light a diya before Tulsi every evening of Kartika month
  • Sing wedding songs with the women of the household and neighborhood

✕ Don'ts

  • Do not pluck Tulsi leaves on Sundays, Ekadashi, or after sunset
  • Do not perform the puja carelessly — this is a full wedding, treat it as one
  • Do not skip the Saptapadi — the seven circumambulations are the heart of the ritual
  • Do not leave Tulsi unwatered on regular days — Vivah requires year-round care
  • Do not forget to distribute prasad to neighbors — the community celebration is essential
