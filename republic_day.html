📖 About

On January 26, 1950, the Constitution of India came into effect — replacing the Government of India Act and making India a fully sovereign, democratic republic. The date was deliberately chosen: on January 26, 1930, the Indian National Congress had declared Purna Swaraj — complete independence — as its goal during the freedom struggle.

Republic Day is not a religious festival. It belongs equally to every Indian regardless of religion, caste, language, or region. It is a day to honor the Constitution, the armed forces, India's extraordinary diversity, and the sacrifices that made this nation free.

"Independence Day marks freedom from British rule. Republic Day marks something equally profound — the moment India gave itself its own set of laws, values, and governance. Dr. B.R. Ambedkar and the Constituent Assembly spent 2 years, 11 months, and 18 days drafting the document. It remains the longest written constitution of any sovereign nation in the world."

📜 The Preamble — Read This Aloud as a Family

The Preamble is one of the most powerful statements of national values ever written. On Republic Day, reading it aloud as a family takes 60 seconds and means everything.

📜 Preamble to the Constitution of India
WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA, having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a SOVEREIGN SOCIALIST SECULAR DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC and to secure to all its citizens:

JUSTICE, social, economic and political;
LIBERTY of thought, expression, belief, faith and worship;
EQUALITY of status and of opportunity;
and to promote among them all FRATERNITY assuring the dignity of the individual and the unity and integrity of the Nation —

IN OUR CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY this twenty-sixth day of November, 1949, do HEREBY ADOPT, ENACT AND GIVE TO OURSELVES THIS CONSTITUTION.

⭐ Significance

Republic Day is a reminder that India is not just a geography — it is an idea. An idea that people of every religion, language, caste, and background can live together as equals under one Constitution, with rights that no king or government can take away. That idea was written down on January 26, 1950. Our job, every year, is to make sure it stays alive.

🎖️ The Republic Day Parade — Kartavya Path, New Delhi

✦ Opening
Flag Unfurling & 21-Gun Salute

The President of India — constitutional head of the nation and Supreme Commander of the Armed Forces — arrives at Kartavya Path. The National Flag is unfurled and the National Anthem plays. 21-gun salute rings out simultaneously. Every Indian present stands at complete attention for the full 52 seconds.

✦ Armed Forces
Military March Past

The parade opens with the armed forces — Army, Navy, and Air Force — marching in immaculate formation, displaying India's military strength and discipline. Regimental bands play as contingents pass the saluting base. The precision and pride in each marching step carries the weight of India's defense.

✦ Most Beloved
State & Ministry Tableaux

Decorated floats from every state and Union Territory, each depicting the culture, heritage, art, and achievements of that region — Kerala's Kathakali, Rajasthan's forts, Assam's one-horned rhino, Gujarat's tribal arts, Ladakh's monasteries. A moving, visual celebration of India's extraordinary diversity passing in a single procession.

✦ Youth & Culture
School Children & Folk Dancers

School children perform synchronized mass drills and cultural displays. NSS and NCC cadets march in formation. Folk dancers from across India — each in their traditional costume — perform on the parade route. It is impossible to watch and not feel the sheer scope of what India contains.

✦ Spectacular
Motorcycle Stunts & BSF Camels

BSF and armed forces jawans perform breathtaking motorcycle stunts — multiple people on a single moving motorcycle in formations. The BSF camel contingent in their ceremonial uniform is a beloved parade tradition. Each display is a testament to the skill and discipline of India's paramilitary forces.

✦ Grand Finale
Air Force Flypast

The parade concludes with the Indian Air Force flypast — fighter jets, helicopters, and transport aircraft flying in formation over Kartavya Path. Often in tricolor formations releasing saffron, white, and green smoke trails across the Delhi sky. The roar of the jets overhead is the punctuation mark of the entire day.

How to watch: Live on Doordarshan (DD National) and all major news channels from ~10 AM, or on YouTube (Doordarshan News channel). Tickets for in-person viewing at Kartavya Path are available via aamantran.mod.gov.in from early January.

🏅 Gallantry Awards — The Heart of Republic Day

One of the most moving parts of Republic Day is the presentation of Gallantry Awards — honors given to military and civilian personnel for acts of extraordinary bravery. When a widow walks up to receive her husband's award posthumously, or a child receives it on behalf of a parent who gave their life — that moment is the true meaning of Republic Day. Watch for these moments in the telecast.

⭐
Param Vir Chakra
India's highest military honor — for the most conspicuous acts of bravery in the face of the enemy. Equivalent to the Victoria Cross or Medal of Honor.
🔵
Ashoka Chakra
The highest peacetime gallantry award — for acts of bravery not in the presence of the enemy. Often awarded to police, CRPF, and intelligence officers.
🟡
Vir Chakra
The third-highest wartime gallantry award — for acts of bravery in the presence of the enemy, on the battlefield or during operations.
🟢
Kirti & Shaurya Chakra
Second and third highest peacetime gallantry awards — for acts of bravery in peacetime operations, counter-insurgency, and civilian heroism.
🫡
Padma Awards
Padma Vibhushan, Padma Bhushan, and Padma Shri — civilian honors announced on Republic Day for extraordinary contributions to arts, science, social service, and public affairs.

🚩 Hoisting the Flag at Home — Correctly

✦ Your Right
Flag Code of India, 2002

Hoisting the National Flag at your home on Republic Day is your right as a citizen — affirmed by the Flag Code of India, 2002. Buy a proper Khadi or polyester flag from a certified vendor, post office, or government-approved seller. The Bureau of Indian Standards has exact specifications — never buy a low-quality paper flag.

✦ Hoisting
Hoist It — Don't Just Hang It

The flag must be hoisted on a pole so it flies freely in the air — not draped limp against a wall. Saffron stripe always at the top. Never upside down (green on top). The Ashoka Chakra must be clearly visible on both sides. The flag is raised at sunrise and brought down at sunset — not left out overnight.

✦ Respect
The Flag Must Be Pristine

A torn, faded, or dirty flag must never be displayed — this is considered disrespectful. Do not let the flag touch the ground at any time during hoisting or lowering. If a flag is damaged or worn out, it must be disposed of privately and with dignity — not thrown in a bin or burned in public.

✦ Community
Society & School Flag Hoisting

Attend your local housing society, school, or office flag hoisting. Stand at complete attention during the National Anthem — feet together, hands by sides, head straight, no movement for the full 52 seconds. After the hoisting, laddoos are distributed to all present — the small, sweet act of sharing that makes the national moment into a community moment.

🎵 Jana Gana Mana — The National Anthem

Composed by Rabindranath Tagore and adopted as the National Anthem on January 24, 1950 — just two days before the first Republic Day. Duration: exactly 52 seconds. Stand straight, at complete attention, for the full 52 seconds. This is the law and the least we can do.

🎨 How to Celebrate — At Home & Community

✦ Creative
Make a Tricolor Rangoli

At your entrance, make a rangoli in saffron, white, and green — using colored powder, flower petals, or rice flour. Place an Ashoka Chakra at the center using blue flowers or powder. A beautiful, creative act of patriotic decoration that transforms your doorstep into a statement.

✦ Community
Cook & Share with Neighbors

Prepare sweets or food and share with neighbors — especially those who live alone, the elderly, or families in need. Sharing food on a national day is a simple but powerful act of community. This is what the Constitution's fraternity looks like in practice — not in words but in a plate of sweets at someone's door.

✦ Most Important
Teach Children the Meaning of Today

Don't let Republic Day pass as just a holiday. Explain to children what the Constitution is, why January 26th matters, who Dr. Ambedkar was, what the fundamental rights are, and why freedom fighters sacrificed what they did. Even five minutes of this conversation is more valuable than any textbook. It is the most important thing you can do today.

✦ Evening
Watch a Patriotic Film

Spend the evening watching a film that honors India's history, freedom struggle, or armed forces — Rang De Basanti, Lagaan, URI: The Surgical Strike, Shershah, The Legend of Bhagat Singh, or 12th Fail (about integrity and perseverance). Cinema carries what speeches cannot — the emotional truth of what this nation has been through.

📌 Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do's

  • Hoist or display the National Flag with full respect and correct orientation (saffron on top)
  • Stand at complete attention — still, straight, silent — for the full 52 seconds of the National Anthem
  • Watch the parade — the tableaux and gallantry awards especially
  • Read the Preamble to the Constitution aloud as a family
  • Attend your local school, society, or community flag hoisting ceremony
  • Teach children what the Constitution is, who Dr. Ambedkar was, and why this day matters
  • Share sweets with neighbors — make the national moment into a community moment

✕ Don'ts

  • Never display a damaged, torn, faded, or dirty flag
  • Never let the flag touch the ground during hoisting or lowering
  • Never sit, talk, or move during the National Anthem
  • Never use the National Flag as decoration on clothing, tablecloths, or disposable items
  • Do not treat Republic Day as just a holiday — it is a day of active, conscious citizenship
  • Never burn or discard the National Flag disrespectfully
