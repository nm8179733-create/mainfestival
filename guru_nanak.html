📖 About

Guru Nanak Jayanti — also called Gurpurab — celebrates the birth of Guru Nanak Dev Ji, the founder of Sikhism and the first of the ten Sikh Gurus, born on April 15, 1469 in Nankana Sahib (present-day Pakistan).

Born into a Hindu family, he showed signs of extraordinary spiritual depth from childhood. At age 30 he disappeared into a river for three days — emerging with a declaration that would change India forever:

"There is no Hindu, there is no Muslim."

Meaning — beyond all religious labels, there is only one God and one humanity. He spent the next 24 years traveling across India, Arabia, Persia, and Tibet — on foot — spreading three core teachings. He established the institution of Langar — the free community kitchen where everyone sits together on the floor and eats the same food regardless of caste, religion, or status. This single institution was the most radical social reform of 15th century India.

⭐ Significance

Guru Nanak Jayanti is the birthday of the man who walked into a river and came out saying there is no Hindu and no Muslim — only God and humanity. Everything else he taught — the Langar, the Kirtan, the equality — flows from that one moment of clarity. On this day we don't just remember him. We ask: do I see humanity the way he did?

💡 The Three Core Teachings

Naam Japo
Meditate on God's Name

Repeat "Waheguru" continuously throughout the day — inhale "Wahe," exhale "Guru." Keep the divine name alive in every breath, not just in prayer time.

Kirat Karo
Earn Honestly

Earn your livelihood through honest work, giving full effort. Reflect today: am I earning honestly? Am I giving my full effort to what I do?

Vand Chhako
Share With Others

Share what you earn with others. Give something today — food, money, time — to someone who needs it. Even feeding one hungry person is Guru Nanak's teaching made real.

📜 Akhand Path — 48 Hours of Recitation

48 hours before Gurpurab, Gurudwaras begin the Akhand Path — continuous, uninterrupted recitation of the entire Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji (1430 pages). Teams of Granthis rotate throughout — it never stops, concluding on Gurpurab morning.

The Akhand Path is not background recitation — it is the Guru's own voice speaking continuously for two full days and nights. The Sangat comes and goes, listening, sitting in the presence of the eternal word.

"Attend the Bhog (conclusion ceremony) on Gurpurab morning — this is the single most important event of the day. The final pages of Guru Granth Sahib Ji are recited, Ardas is performed, Hukamnama is taken, and Karah Prasad is distributed."

🎭 Activities & Ceremony

✦ Amrit Vela
Wake Before Dawn

Rise at Amrit Vela — 2 to 6 AM. Take full bath (Ishnaan). Wear white or blue clothes. Cover head. This early rising is not optional — it is the beginning of Gurpurab.

✦ Nitnem
Recite the Five Daily Prayers

Japji Sahib · Jaap Sahib · Tav Prasad Savaiye · Chaupai Sahib · Anand Sahib. Then perform Ardas — stand with folded hands and offer your morning prayer specifically remembering Guru Nanak Dev Ji.

"Waheguru Ji Ka Khalsa, Waheguru Ji Ki Fateh"
✦ Gurudwara
Enter with Full Protocol

Remove shoes. Remove all leather. Wash hands and feet. Cover head completely. Enter quietly. Perform Matha Tekna — go on both knees and touch forehead to the floor before Guru Granth Sahib Ji.

✦ Bhog
Akhand Path Conclusion

Attend the Bhog ceremony — the conclusion of the 48-hour recitation. Final pages read, Ardas performed, Hukamnama taken. The Guru's divine word for the day is declared to the entire Sangat.

✦ Prasad
Receive Karah Prasad

Cup both hands together. Receive warm Karah Prasad — wheat halwa made from equal parts flour, ghee, and sugar. Eating it is a sacred act of receiving the Guru's blessing without distinction of caste or status.

✦ Kirtan
Sit in Gurbani Kirtan

Sit in the Sangat and absorb devotional singing of Gurbani in classical Ragas. On Gurpurab, Shabads composed by or about Guru Nanak Dev Ji are sung. Even sitting quietly is a complete act of worship.

✦ Nagar Kirtan
The Grand Procession

Led by the Panj Pyare in blue Khalsa attire. Guru Granth Sahib Ji carried on a decorated Palki Sahib. Gatka (Sikh martial art) performed. Thousands chant Gurbani through city streets.

✦ Langar Seva
Volunteer in the Community Kitchen

Chop vegetables, make rotis, serve food, wash dishes. This seva is the most direct honoring of Guru Nanak's teaching of Vand Chhako. Every person who sits on that floor and eats is an equal — Guru Nanak's revolution in action.

🙏 Key Prayers & Mantras

🪯
Mool Mantar — The Root of All Sikh Prayer
"Ik Onkar, Sat Naam, Karta Purakh, Nirbhau, Nirvair
Akaal Moorat, Ajooni Saibhang, Gur Prasad. Jap."
One God exists. Truth is His name. He is the Creator. Without fear. Without enmity. Timeless. Beyond birth and death. Self-illuminated. Realized by Guru's grace. Meditate.
✨
Simran — Repeat Continuously All Day
"Waheguru, Waheguru, Waheguru..."
Inhale "Wahe" — exhale "Guru." The wondrous Lord. Not a ritual — a way of breathing. Guru Nanak taught that God's name should be woven into every breath, not reserved for prayer time.
🤝
Sikh Greeting — Say to Everyone Today
"Waheguru Ji Ka Khalsa, Waheguru Ji Ki Fateh"
The Khalsa belongs to the Wondrous Lord. Victory belongs to the Wondrous Lord. Not a greeting of rank or status — a declaration that everyone you meet belongs to God.
🌍
Guru Nanak's Most Famous Verse — Closing Line of Every Ardas
"Nanak Naam Chardi Kala, Tere Bhane Sarbat Da Bhala"
Nanak — with God's name and high spirits — may Your will bring goodness to all of humanity. This ends every Sikh prayer. Not a prayer for personal blessing — a prayer for all of humanity. This is who Guru Nanak was.

📌 Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do's

  • Wake at Amrit Vela and recite full Nitnem
  • Cover head at all times in the Gurudwara
  • Attend Akhand Path Bhog — the most important event of the day
  • Perform Matha Tekna before Guru Granth Sahib Ji
  • Do Langar Seva — volunteer without fail
  • Walk in or respectfully observe the Nagar Kirtan
  • Greet all Sikhs with Waheguru Ji Ka Khalsa, Waheguru Ji Ki Fateh
  • Practice Naam Japo, Kirat Karo, Vand Chhako consciously today

✕ Don'ts

  • No leather items anywhere in the Gurudwara
  • Never sit with feet pointing toward Guru Granth Sahib Ji
  • Never turn your back to Guru Granth Sahib Ji when leaving
  • Do not enter without covering your head
  • Do not treat Gurpurab as only a religious holiday — it is a call to live differently
  • Do not skip Langar Seva — Guru Nanak's teaching requires action, not just attendance
